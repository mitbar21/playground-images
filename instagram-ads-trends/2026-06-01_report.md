## 5. Cross-account winning themes

Five themes repeated across the set, and they cluster tightly around **creative strategy and account structure**, not bidding tricks:

1. **Creative pillars beat single winners (the Andromeda mental model).** The strongest theme of the scan. Under Meta's current algorithm, scaling comes from running several distinct creative *concepts* — each reaching audiences the others can't — and building real versions inside each, not copying one winner. This carried the highest engagement in the entire set.
2. **The funnel, not the ad, is the unit.** Multiple creators reframed "expensive ads" as a missing-layers problem: educate cold traffic, stack trust in the middle, drive retention/LTV at the bottom. Operators are being told to build ecosystems, not ad accounts.
3. **Simple, single-job creative wins.** Two separate angles landed here — "let the product do the talking" and "each winning brand masters one creative lever." The shared truth: overloaded, overproduced ads lose to clean creative that communicates one idea in three seconds.
4. **Structure protects performance.** The "new ads into new ad sets" rule and the "don't touch the winning ad set" instinct both say the same thing — careless launches reset learning and crash campaigns. Testing discipline is being framed as the real scaling lever.
5. **Google: the damage is in the defaults (under-represented but present).** The lone strong Google angle was a service-business setup walk-through whose core value was turning *off* Google's default Display + Search Partners networks, tightening location, and scheduling to business hours. **Platform balance note:** the tracked set leans Meta, and Google creators (@benheathmarketing, @aaronyoungofficial) had low IG engagement this window, so Google is thin. If you want more Google depth, those two are better mined on YouTube. Hashtag search surfaced only low-signal accounts (SMM panels, tiny agencies) — the tracked set carried this scan entirely.

### Script 1: One winner isn't a strategy

**Topic:** Why copying your one winning ad stalls the account — and why creative pillars scale instead.
**Pillar:** Structural Tear-Down
**Hook Type:** Symptom to Root Cause
**Format:** Hybrid — camera hook → Meta Ad Library teardown → camera close

**Source video:** https://www.instagram.com/p/DTI_VewD2Lk/ — @sampiliero
**Source structure breakdown:** Opens with a then-vs-now contrast on how Meta serves ads (audience-first → concept-first under Andromeda). Body is an ad-library teardown of one big brand, pulling out three distinct creative pillars (templated product splash, product-in-use, raw UGC) and showing each reaches an audience the others can't. Closes on the reframe that tiny tweaks are dead but pillar concepts compound.

**On-screen hook text:** ONE WINNER ISN'T A STRATEGY
**Spoken hook:** "Your ad account stalls because every creative is chasing the same buyer."
**Hold line:** "You found one winner, copied it ten ways, and growth flatlined."

**Hybrid script:**
- **[0–3s · CAMERA]** *Hook:* "Your ad account stalls because every creative is chasing the same buyer."
- **[3–6s · CAMERA]** *Hold:* "You found one winner, copied it ten ways, and growth flatlined."
- **[6–14s · SCREEN — pull up a big brand's Meta Ad Library, scroll slowly]** VO: "Watch this. Real ad library, billion-dollar brand. Look at what they're actually running."
- **[14–28s · SCREEN — highlight three clusters one at a time]** VO: "These — clean product on a templated background. One concept. *(highlight next)* These — product out in the real world, big headline. Different feel, different buyer. *(highlight next)* And these — raw customer video. Three concepts, three audiences that'd never convert on each other's ads."
- **[28–37s · SCREEN — scroll the many variations inside one cluster]** VO: "Here's what everyone misses. They're not hunting for the one winner. They picked a concept that works and built ten versions of it."
- **[37–43s · CAMERA]** *Close:* "Copies of one ad plateau. Concepts stacked on concepts scale. That's the whole game now."

**Capture notes:** Screen-record any large brand's Meta Ad Library; blur the brand name. Group the ads visually into the 3 pillars as you talk.

**Why this hook should perform:** Names a symptom every advertiser has lived — scale stops even though the ad "works" — then *shows* the fix in a real account instead of describing it. The library teardown is the proof that makes the reframe land.
