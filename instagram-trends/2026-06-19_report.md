## Cross-account winning themes

**Theme 1: Self-correcting loops and agentic autonomy**
The single biggest engagement cluster this cycle. @itsmariahbrunner's loops post (3,650 comments), @duncanrogoff's RuFlow (28,490 comments), and @claudeai's Fable 5 (97K likes) are all making the same structural argument from different angles: the AI agent shouldn't wait for you to check its work. Whether it's a single-thread loop with a measurable goal, a 60-agent team that routes and self-improves, or a Fable 5 deployment that runs for days unsupervised, the winning content is framing AI as a system that closes its own loops — not a smarter assistant you have to manage. This theme has comment-trigger depth (people want the setup, not just the concept) and is clearly early — most builders are still in the prompt-and-check mode it's calling out.

**Theme 2: Claude Cowork as a full solo operation**
@sabrina_ramonov (23K comments) and @advicewithjean (10.6K comments) are both running the same structural argument: one person plus Claude Cowork equals a full department. Sabrina's angle is content production (32M views, /post command, 8 platforms); Jean's angle is data-driven growth (Cowork auditing Instagram, recycling top content, dashboard). Both are driving comment-trigger CTAs because the proof is specific and the setup is transferable. The theme is expanding from content into anything that was previously outsourced — agencies, coaches, strategists. Expect more creators to hit this angle as Cowork adoption grows.

**Theme 3: Cost elimination in the Claude Code stack**
@nick_saraev's free Claude Code post (19,344 comments) and his efficiency post (1,720 likes / 708 comments, /model OpusPlan + /compact + ultrathink) signal a growing audience of builders looking to run Claude Code at lower cost without degraded output. The rerouting-to-free-models angle is the strongest (social proof: 20K devs) but the efficiency tricks angle has real staying power because it applies to paid users who can't or won't reroute. This is a durable theme with a large addressable audience.

**Theme 4: Fable 5 and the "too capable to regulate" moment**
Multiple creators are covering the same story from different angles: Fable 5 launched, Mythos was shut down by the government 3 days later, the capability is real enough to be a national security concern. @duncanrogoff, @sabrina_ramonov, @nocode.joshua, and several search-surfaced accounts all touched this in the past 7 days. The builder-facing angle (what does "operates for days without supervision" actually unlock?) is the content gap that's still open — most coverage is newsy, not operational.

**Theme 5: Skills and plugins as specialist team deployment**
@itsmariahbrunner's 24-installs post (2,205 comments) and @nick_saraev's Claude Skills library post (4,109 comments, from scan history) are both landing well. The framing: skills and plugins aren't features, they're specialist team members you install once. Content that lists specific skills with clear use cases (Brand Voice, PDF Viewer, Operations, Customer Support) and gives a CTA to get the setup is converting to comments at a strong rate. The audience is clearly interested in expanding Claude's functional surface area beyond chat.

## Quick execution notes

The four highest-leverage scripts to record first are 1, 2, 3, and 10 — they cover the two dominant themes (loops and multi-agent systems) and the Cowork operating-system angle that's generating the most comment volume right now. Record Script 1 and Script 9 back-to-back in the same session since they're the same source and the second is a natural follow-up — but stagger the posts by 3–4 days so they don't cannibalize each other.

On delivery: the scripts are written to be spoken, not read. The most common tell when recording is slowing down at the hold line — keep the energy flat and blunt from the hook all the way through. Mitch's real cadence is slightly breathless and slightly sarcastic, not deliberate or instructive. If a line sounds like a presentation, speed through it. If the close sounds like a slogan, cut the last word and let the silence land.

Comment-trigger CTAs are built into every script. Pick one trigger word per script and commit to it before filming — having it in the caption and saying it on camera are both necessary for the DM funnel to fire.

Scripts 8 and 6 are the only two that don't have a comment trigger — both close on a question instead. These are better for saves and profile visits than comment volume, so plan them as middle-of-feed posts rather than top-of-funnel reach drivers.
