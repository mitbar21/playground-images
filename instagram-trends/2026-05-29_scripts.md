### Script 9: The Finger Test

The method for catching AI-generated video — and why even that method is starting to fail.

**Source video**
https://www.instagram.com/p/DIXB67gTjPF/ — @treysmith

**Source structure breakdown**
Transcript available. Hook drops the reveal without warning — the video appears to be showing something real, then cuts to "That's all AI. None of that is real." Body is a methodical real-time walkthrough: count the fingers (six, not five), watch the hand morph mid-frame as it repositions on a microphone, notice the lip sync quality. Named mechanism: the visual inspection method. Close is cultural commentary with no CTA — "We are absolutely cooked here." Stakes without instruction. The energy is casual and reactive, not educational.

**Hook**
There are three things you can check to tell if a video is AI-generated. I'm going to walk you through all three — and then show you a clip where all three pass.

**Hold line**
That's the part worth paying attention to.

**Body**
First is fingers. AI still adds extra digits in complex hand positions — six fingers, sometimes seven if the hand is doing something complicated like holding an object. You zoom in and count.

Second is morphing. AI-generated hands and faces will shift slightly when the frame changes — a hand holding a mic at one angle repositions into a slightly different grip in the next cut. It's subtle but consistent. You pause and look at the transition.

Third is lip sync. Most AI video struggles with consonants at the edges of a sentence — you'll see the mouth close a beat early or stay open too long on the last word.

Here's the problem: there's a video circulating right now where I ran all three checks. The fingers were correct. The hand transitions held. The lip sync was clean.

When your best detection method starts returning false negatives, the question shifts. It's not "how do I spot AI" anymore. It's "what do I do when I can't?"

**Close**
I don't think the answer is checking harder. I think it's deciding in advance what you'd do if the person you're talking to isn't who they say they are.
