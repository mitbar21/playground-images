## Cross-account winning themes

**Theme 1: "One person + Claude = an entire team"** is the dominant story of this cycle. Multiple creators — including posts from @itsmariahbrunner, @aiagently, and the reference post from @sabrina_ramonov — are all publishing variations of "I built my entire marketing team / content department inside Claude" with comment volumes ranging from 1,400 to 23,600. The framing has moved from "Claude is useful" to "Claude is the staff." The content that's winning treats Claude as infrastructure that replaced external hires, not a tool you pick up occasionally. Comment engagement (not just likes) is the leading signal: 23K comments on Sabrina's Cowork reel and 6.8K on Mariah's PDF-to-skill post prove operators are acting on these prompts.

**Theme 2: Claude Code orchestration and efficiency** is the breakout technical angle. Duncan Rogoff's RuFlow reel (49K likes, 30K comments) introduced a concrete mechanic most operators hadn't seen: automatic routing of simple tasks to cheap models and complex tasks to powerful ones, running in a 60+-agent system. The angle "you don't need one big expensive brain for everything" is actionable and directly addresses the token-cost anxiety that's growing as Claude usage scales.

**Theme 3: Claude Tag in Slack as a real teammate** is the freshest story in the set (June 23). The official @claudeai post drove 19K likes and was immediately picked up by multiple secondary accounts. The angle that separates it from prior integrations: Claude has its own Slack account with its own credentials and scoped per-channel permissions, so it genuinely cannot see data it wasn't given access to. That security framing is new and has not yet been widely translated into practical operator content.

**Theme 4: Converting existing content into runnable Claude skills** is having a breakout week. Mariah's PDF-to-skill reel hit 6.8K comments on only 2K likes — the highest execution-intent ratio in the entire scan. The underlying pain is clear: operators have folders of saved guides, tutorials, and frameworks they never act on. The mechanism (PDF in, skill out, runs on command) turns passive consumption into active infrastructure. No other creator this week framed it this cleanly.

Record the scripts in the order listed — the template rotation (V/S/M/ML/T/V/S/M/T/ML) means no two consecutive reels look or feel the same to viewers scrolling your feed. The "team" scripts (scripts 2, 6, 10) can be batched in one session if you want to ride the "Claude as staff" wave while engagement on that theme is still peaking.
