Graphify frames it as a context problem — stop re-reading your codebase every session. Both posts drove 10K–27K comments, signaling this is a genuine pain point, not just curiosity. The "cut costs 50%, extend usage 250%" outcome language outperformed every other hook format this week.

**Theme 2 — Claude Cowork as the solo operator's force multiplier.** Sabrina Ramonov's "32M views solo, one command, 8 platforms" post (22K comments) and advicewithjean's "Claude audited my Instagram" post (10K comments) are the same thesis from two angles: one person with the right Cowork setup is replacing what used to require a team. The engagement pattern — both posts topping 10K comments via comment-trigger CTAs — shows the audience is actively pulling on this topic, not just watching. The pain they're resolving is real: content production cost and the feeling of being outrun by people with bigger teams.

**Theme 3 — Fable 5 launch creates an operator readiness test.** The official claudeai post (659K views, 92K likes) plus duncanrogoff's Fable 5/Ultra Code reaction (same day) represent the biggest model event of the scan period. The angle that cuts through is not "Fable 5 is powerful" but "your workflows aren't designed to use it." Days-long autonomous operation is the specific capability that exposes a gap: operators whose AI agents require constant supervision are now architecturally behind. This "readiness test" framing outperforms pure feature-announcement copy.

**Theme 4 — AI ROI with receipts beats AI hype.** Itsmariahbrunner's post — opening with "people love to say AI changed their life with zero proof" — pulled 2K comments from an account with modest view counts. The proof structure (specific numbers: followers, brand deal revenue, expense reduction percentages) resonated because the space is saturated with vague AI transformation claims. The "30-day AI challenge" launching June 22 will likely generate high comment volume. This is a useful counter-angle: lead with documented proof, not possibility.

**Theme 5 — AI content authenticity as both risk and opportunity.** Two converging signals: Wikipedia publishing AI writing giveaways (nocode.joshua, 1K comments) and treysmith's "is this real?" AI video going viral (25M views). The former is a risk signal for content operators (your AI output is getting caught). The latter is an opportunity signal — the gap between "real" and "AI-generated" is functionally closed at the video level, creating leverage for operators who move now. Both angles hit different sides of the same underlying shift.
