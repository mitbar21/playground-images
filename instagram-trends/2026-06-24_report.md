## Cross-account winning themes

Three themes are dominating this week across all tracked accounts.

**Theme 1: Model routing and token efficiency.** The highest-engagement post of the entire cycle is duncanrogoff's RuFlow video (48.7K likes, 30.3K comments) — and its core mechanic is automatic routing: cheap models handle simple work, expensive models handle complex work. Nick Saraev's free Claude Code post (21.6K likes, 19.6K comments) runs the same argument from a different angle: you do not have to use your paid plan for every request. Both posts exploded because Claude usage limits and credit burn are the single biggest friction point builders face right now. Any script that opens on that pain lands immediately.

**Theme 2: Claude as a complete operating system once properly set up.** Sabrina Ramonov's 23.4K-comment marketing department post, itsmariahbrunner's PDF-to-skill and Artifacts posts (3.6K-6.1K comments), nocode.joshua's 33.3K-comment starter setup video, and advicewithjean's IG audit post all share the same underlying message: most people are using Claude like a chat box. The ones getting results are building it as a system with memory, skills, and real context. The packaging shifts per creator but the angle is consistent. Scripts framing Claude as an operating system rather than a chat tool are outperforming by 3-5x on comment engagement.

**Theme 3: Claude entering team infrastructure with scoped permissions.** Claude Tag launched June 23 and immediately drove 16.5K likes on the official account. Itsmariahbrunner covered Claude in Slack with 440 comments the same day. The per-channel permission model, the bounded memory, and Claude having its own account are the specific angles getting engagement. The broader creator audience is catching up to enterprise-grade Claude deployment. Scripts that break down what the permission architecture actually means for operators and team leads are landing in a moment when nobody else is explaining it.

All scripts are written to be said not read — if a line sounds like you're reading it, slow down and say it in your own words from the bullet point. The close question is the most important line. Land it flat. Don't let it drift up into a question-inflection at the end or it sounds soft.
