That's the most borrowable agentic framing available, and it's broader-audience than tutorial content. **(3) Cost and lock-in anxiety.** "Run Claude Code on open models," "open-source Claude Design, any model, no cap" — contrast hooks (keep the workflow, kill the bill / drop the lock-in) still pulling 14–17K comments. **(4) Talk-to-your-documents.** Sci-Hub's Sci-Bot put a mainstream face on retrieval-with-citations, which maps directly onto pointing Claude at your own files. **(5) Opus 4.8 is the fresh news hook.** The model launch is just hitting creator content (Mariah's cheat-sheet and scorecard posts), and the under-covered real story is agent reliability across long multi-step tasks. The common mechanic across all five: **contrast or stakes in the first line, a named Claude surface or capability in the second, one concrete mechanism in the body** — not tip lists.
