## Cross-account winning themes

**Theme 1 — The one-operator model is the dominant conversion angle.** Sabrina's 22K-comment Cowork reel and Jean's 9.9K-comment IG audit are the two highest-engagement practitioner posts in this scan. Both frame the same core idea: a single operator with Claude can replace an entire team or agency function. The comment volume proves the idea triggers direct action, not passive watching. Any script that delivers a specific "job your team used to do that Claude now runs" example will land in this environment.

**Theme 2 — Free access unlocks are driving outsized comment volume.** Nick Saraev's three most-commented posts this cycle are all "here's the free version you didn't know existed" — free Claude Code via GitHub (17.5K comments), free Open Design (14K comments), free website build from prompt library (8.8K comments). The audience is not just price-sensitive; they're responding to the "the locked thing just opened" narrative. Builder-specific unlock content outperforms general AI tips in comment count by a significant margin.

**Theme 3 — The "Claude reads your own data back to you" angle is underexplored.** Jean's Claude Cowork IG audit post is the only example in this scan of an agent operating on the creator's own historical data to generate a forward plan. The engagement (9.9K comments) is stronger than most pure-tutorial posts. This is a pattern worth repeating across other data sources: email inbox analysis, client pipeline review, sales call transcripts.

**Theme 4 — AI output quality (the "slop" problem) is an active pain.** Joshua's Claude + Google Stitch website post (917 comments, May 25), Nick's 3D website via MotionSites prompt library (8.8K comments, May 20), and Mariah's Claude design posts all circle the same problem: AI output looks generic unless you give it a visual or structural scaffold first. The fix is specific and demonstrable — scripts that show the scaffold step outperform scripts that just say "use better prompts."

**Theme 5 — AI sovereignty and local-first execution is entering the mainstream.** PewDiePie's Odysseus (79.7K likes, June 2) and NVIDIA's RTX Spark (66.9K likes, June 1) reaching millions via @evolving.ai signals that "run AI on your own machine" is becoming a mass conversation. Practitioner content that gets ahead of this — specifically around what it means for agent deployment and client data security — will find an audience primed by the cultural context.
