## Cross-account winning themes

**Theme 1 — Claude Cowork as autonomous operator.** The single most-repeated pattern this cycle: one person using Cowork to replace an agency, content team, or workflow stack. Sabrina's /post command video (21.5K comments) and Jean's computer use + Instagram analytics video (9.4K comments) both use the same structural move — name the old way, name what it cost (money or time), then show the new command. The credibility gap between "AI can help with content" and "AI runs my entire content operation" is closing, and creators who put a concrete number on it (32M views, 100K followers in 20 days) are driving the highest comment volumes. The comment trigger "COWORK" is now a confirmed high-conversion mechanism across at least two large accounts.

**Theme 2 — Free/open alternatives to paid Claude tools.** Nick Saraev hit his two highest-engagement posts of the cycle with this exact frame: free Claude Code via GitHub routing (15.6K comments) and Open Design as a free Claude Design replacement (13.2K comments). The pattern is: name the paid tool, name the open-source mirror, reduce friction (five seconds to set up, works with your existing subscription). This works because Claude's tools are genuinely high-value and the "zero cost" unlock activates strong sharing behavior. Nocode.Joshua echoed the Claude Code + Stitch angle on May 25 — one day before this scan — confirming the theme is still building, not fading.

**Theme 3 — Comment-trigger lead magnet as the dominant distribution mechanic.** Every video with more than 5K comments in this scan ends with "comment X and I'll DM you Y." The comment-to-like ratio is the signal: Sabrina's codes video at 27,985 comments / 50,960 likes (55% ratio) and her Cowork video at 21,496 comments / 4,970 likes (433% ratio) show the mechanic working at two different scales. The offer has to be specific — not "more content," but a direct link, a prompt, a guide, a specific thing named in the video. The mechanic itself is now table stakes for short-form creator distribution in this niche.

**Theme 4 — Mode-trigger and command-word framing.** Sabrina's ChatGPT codes video is the breakout data point: 28K comments on a numbered list of mode-trigger words that shift how the model responds. The framing ("secret codes") isn't technically accurate but it is compelling — it implies hidden functionality most people haven't unlocked. The angle translates directly to Claude and has not been executed with Claude branding at the scale this scan reveals. The structural gap is real and immediate.
