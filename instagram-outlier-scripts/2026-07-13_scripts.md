## Script 1 — "AI made me 114 ad creatives for under a dollar — and 110 of them would've torched your budget"
*Source: https://www.instagram.com/reel/Dani0pLNetC/ (@sammy.vidrain — 47.9x median). Format: selfie hook → fast screen-record, ~40s.*

**Hook (on-screen + said):** "AI just spun me up a hundred and fourteen ad creatives for under a dollar — and about a hundred and ten of them would've quietly burned your budget."

**Body:** "And I'm not knocking it, the tech is unreal, you connect the thing, type one line, and it hands you more UGC ads than a full production team makes in a month, for the price of a pack of gum. That part's solved. Here's the part nobody's selling you: volume was never the problem. Picking was the problem. Out of those hundred and fourteen, four had a hook worth spending real money behind, and if you can't tell which four before you fund them, you don't have a creative machine, you have a money shredder that runs fast. We plug this exact workflow into client accounts — but the AI makes the hundred, we decide which four go live, and last month that one decision took a home-services account from a $71 lead to a $23 one."

**Close:** "The cheap part is making them. The expensive part is knowing which ones deserve your money — and that read is the whole game."

*Visual note: screen-record the AI cranking out a grid of creatives, then hard-cut to 4 highlighted. Stat popups top-zone only: "$0.89 / 114 ads" then "4 worth funding." Captions lower third, white only.*

## Script 2 — "Everybody's bragging they're the only human left in their company"

**Hook (on-screen + said):** "Everybody's out here bragging they're the only human left in their company — right up until the ad account gets throttled and there's nobody in the building who knows why."

**Body:** "I've watched the demos, the little agent org chart, the bots talking to each other 24/7, and honestly it's cool as hell for the stuff that's repeatable — pulling reports, drafting captions, chasing cold leads. Let 'em run. But paid ads isn't repeatable. The platform changes the rules on a Tuesday with no warning, a winning campaign starts bleeding for a reason that isn't in any dashboard, and an agent will happily keep spending into a dumpster fire because the numbers technically still compute. It doesn't panic. It doesn't smell it. We had a client whose automated setup 'looked healthy' while it quietly torched twelve grand — because the agent optimized the wrong event and nothing flagged it. A human who'd seen it before caught it in ten minutes."

**Close:** "Automate the hands all day. Just don't hand the checkbook to something that's never felt an account go sideways."

## Script 3 — "One product, five angles, and the winning ad is never the one you'd bet on"
*Source: https://www.instagram.com/reel/DaiheORzha0/ (@onmywaypodcast_ — 123.4x median). Format: split-screen / talking head with b-roll, ~45s.*

**Hook (on-screen + said):** "One product, five different angles, and I promise you the winning ad is never the one you'd have bet the house on."

**Body:** "Take anything — say a coffee. You can sell it on taste, on productivity, on convenience, on price, on the morning ritual, that's five completely different ads for the same cup. Most people write the one angle they personally love, run it, and when it flops they blame the product. Wrong move. You run all five, small, same budget, and then you shut your mouth and let the metrics talk — cost per click, click-through, cost per lead. The account tells you who your buyer actually is, and it's almost never who you thought. We did this for a client dead sure their thing sold on 'premium quality' — the quality angle died, the 'save two hours a day' angle came in at half the cost, and we 2x'd their return by building the whole business around the angle they didn't like."

**Close:** "Your opinion doesn't scale accounts. The angle the data picks does — so test five and get out of your own way."

*Visual note: numbered 1–5 list of angles as top-zone popups, then a stat card showing the "loser" angle winning on CPL. Real mockups of the 5 ad variations, not icons.*

## Script 5 — "You can clone a full AI ad system for free this weekend — the free part is the trap"
*Source: https://www.instagram.com/reel/Daf1TC8oc3q/ (@structurewebworks — 58.5x median, second angle). Format: talking head + b-roll of the build, ~40s.*

**Hook (on-screen + said):** "You can clone an entire AI ad system for free this weekend, and the 'free' part is exactly the trap they're not warning you about."

**Body:** "Because building it has never been easier — copy the prompts, wire the connectors, boom, you've got an agent that'll launch campaigns and write copy by Sunday night. Feels like you unlocked something. But nobody got rich owning a stack. They got rich on the thousand tiny decisions the stack can't make for you — which audience is actually worth it, when to kill a set that's 'fine,' how much to push a winner before it tips, what a real lead looks like versus a form-fill that ghosts. The tool is free. The judgment that makes the tool profitable took years to build and it doesn't come in the download. We've watched people spin up the same system we use and still bleed, because they had the hands and skipped the reps."

**Close:** "The system's free. Knowing what to do when it's losing money — that's the part you can't screenshot."

*Visual note: b-roll of a build/setup screen-record, then a flat 'downloaded ≠ profitable' beat. Keep energy up, wider framing, raw-timeline flubs fine.*

## Script 6 — "The agencies winning right now aren't making better ads"
*Source: https://www.instagram.com/reel/Dani0pLNetC/ (@sammy.vidrain — 47.9x median, second angle: volume + reads). Format: talking head, ~40s.*

**Hook (on-screen + said):** "The people crushing paid ads right now aren't making better ads — they're making a hundred mediocre ones and letting the data find the one that prints."

**Body:** "That's the whole shift. It used to be you'd sweat over three perfect creatives because each one cost real time and money to produce. Now AI makes a hundred while you drink your coffee, so the bottleneck moved — it's not 'can you make the ad' anymore, it's 'can you read the account fast enough to catch the winner while it's still cheap.' Volume is free. The read is not. And most people drown in the hundred ads because they never built the muscle to kill ninety-six of them without flinching. We push serious creative volume through client accounts every week — the AI makes it, but the reason it works is somebody knows what a winner smells like on day two, not day twelve."

**Close:** "Anybody can make a hundred ads now. Very few can look at the hundred and know which one to bet on — start building that eye."
